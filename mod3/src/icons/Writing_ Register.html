Информационный текст

Whatever essay type you will choose to write on the exam, it is essential that it is written in a formal academic style and its tone is neutral and thoughtful so avoids extreme opinions or feelings of excitement, anger etc. The academic formal style has distinctive and defining features. Thus, an academic essay 


HAS DOESN'T HAVE
* complete sentences
* formal vocabulary including words from academic wordlists (AWL)
* numbers from one to ten written in words
* the full form of verbs (discuss, improve, continue)
* formal grammar structures, such as nominalisation (the data has been analysed)
* impersonal structures (there is) 
* passive voice
* exact figures if possible
* hedging (seems to, probably, might)
* formal linkers (In addition, Moreover, Consequently) 		 * contractions (it’s, wouldn’t, etc)
* colloquial, spoken language, and slang (gonna, U, dude, kids)
*  phrasal verbs (bring up, get better, get worse, go on) 
* too many simple sentences (Unemployment is a global problem. It concerns many people.)
* rhetorical questions
* vague words (thing, kind of, about thirty students, so on, etc.)
* clichés and idioms1 (a piece of cake, every coin has two sides)   
*personal pronouns 
* adverbs that express personal feelings (luckily, wonderfully, wrong, bad) 
 * quotes (Life is like riding a bicycle) 
*  expressions addressing a reader as “you” 
* generalisations
* bullet points


1 There are academic idioms which can be used in formal texts. For example, trial and error; in the long/short run; across the board; the driving force behind something; to bear something in mind; the last resort.
